About Marguerite Valentine

Marguerite's childhood was spent on the move. From Birmingham to Cardiff to Essex, to Norfolk [two separate places], followed by London. She is now settled in Bristol. She sees herself as a city person who also loves the tranquillity of the English countryside, the wild energy of the Atlantic, the unpredictability of the Lake district and the moodiness of the hills and mountains of Scotland. She says my background gave me an opportunity to recognise how different we all are, but paradoxically how alike we are. Her writing expresses an appreciation of place and a need to understand why people behave in certain ways.

When she's not writing, she's thinking about interior design, describing her style as bohemian chic, reading poetry, modern and not so modern, listening to the wonderful and talented jazz musicians who live and work in Bristol, watching psychological films, and late night surreal 'stand up' comedians.

Talk Therapy: A Radical History for Troubled Times

With Diana Turner, Steve Baker and Susan Budnick.

Narrative nonfiction uses the style of fiction to communicate in an accessible way issues usually found in nonfiction. So for example, In Talk Therapy: A Radical History for Troubled Times, the author considers the interrelationship between an individual and society. She writes 'Psychotherapy is like a piece of improvised music. It has no preconceived stopping or starting point and at its best, its potential is profoundly creative and therapeutic.'

The Arbours Association is a radical psychotherapy organisation which was established in London in the early seventies. The book describes the training of an Arbours therapist. Milieu therapy is compared with the traditional analytic perspective of one- to- one. Finally, the author points to the psychic usefulness of certain iconic films, some of which contribute to a deeper understanding of social and personal problems.

Fiction

Between the Shadow and the Soul

Between the Shadow and the Soul is Marguerite's first novel. Set in London and Jura, a bleak Hebridean Island, it tells the story of Flori who in a moment of madness steals a baby. Relying on the love and affection of Rose, her best friend, and Matt, her lover, both are drawn into her madness. But eventually find themselves faced with the traumatic truth of her past, and are confronted with a terrible dilemma.

Truth and Lies

Truth and Lies is a sequel to Between the Shadow and Soul. Based in London, Wales and Greece, the book follows through the dilemmas of the characters first encountered in Between the Shadow and Soul. What motivates people to lie and behave the way they do, and can they ever be trusted?

My Name is Echo

My Name is Echo is told in the style of magical phantasy. Set in Wales and London, it's the story of a feisty young woman with a problematic relationship with her mother. An absent father leaves her open to a wild infatuation with a Welsh poet. But life is never simple, and Echo finds her own unique way of challenging and facing up to life's problems.

Nonfiction

A series of original papers which consider some of the complexities of being a psychotherapist. Intended for therapists, counsellors, mental health workers and anyone interested in the therapeutic process and perspective, a number of topics are considered. These range through ethical, political, theoretical issues but also point to the use of certain films as a way of making sense of complex behaviour.
